Lessons from Steve Jobs’ Leadership

Reflecting on Steve Jobs’s impactful leadership and visionary approach. by Walter Isaacson

Steve Jobs' legacy as an entrepreneurial innovator is monumental. He co-founded Apple in 1976 in his parents' garage, faced significant challenges, including being ousted in 1985. He returned to Apple in 1997, rescuing it from near collapse and transforming it into one of the world's most valuable companies by the time of his death in October 2011. Jobs’ influence spanned multiple industries including personal computing, animated films, music, smartphones, tablets, retail, and digital publishing. He is remembered alongside other great American innovators like Edison, Ford, and Disney, not for personal flaws but for his transformative contributions to technology and business.

Those who are crazy enough to think they can change the world are the ones who do.
—From Apple's "Think Different" campaign, 1997

Reflecting on the final months with Steve Jobs, he shared insights about his leadership style. Despite his harsh approach, he believed in the talent of his team, saying, "Look at the results." His confidence in his team’s capability and his own drive for excellence led to a series of groundbreaking products, including the iMac, iPod, iTunes, Apple Stores, MacBook, iPhone, iPad, and more. Jobs was surrounded by a dedicated team and family, proving his lasting impact and loyalty.
